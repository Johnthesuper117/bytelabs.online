An informative documant on the Bushido Code


Bushidō (武士道 "the way of the warrior") was a code of conduct for the samurai class and has multiple variations that differ slightly between clans, but they usually include these 7 or 8 virtues:

  • Honor (Meiyo): A samurai's most important virtue, encompassing personal integrity and a strong sense of duty.
  • Loyalty (Chūgai): Unwavering devotion to one's lord, family, and country.
  • Courage (Giri): Bravery in the face of danger and adversity, not just physical but also moral.
  • Self-Discipline: Strict adherence to rules and principles, both in training and in life.
  • Respect (Rei): Courtesy and politeness towards others, especially those of higher status.
  • Honesty (Shin): Truthfulness and integrity in all dealings.
  • Benevolence (Jin): Compassion and kindness towards others.
  • Rectitude (Gi): Acting with justice and righteousness.
